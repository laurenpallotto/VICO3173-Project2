Best of South East Wales

Back in 2017, I was fortunate enough to take a trip with my family to visit my older brother at college. He was in his first year at the Royal Welsh College of Music and Drama in Cardiff. My brother took us all ocer the city to some of hos favorite places that he had gone to during his first few months of college. The entire trip was surreal as we spent some time in the city and contry side durring our 10 days there. With all of the different places that we went on the trip, these are my top 5 favorite. These ar ethe palces that I felt a connection with when I was there and would encourage anyone to add them to their trip.

Cardiff Castle

At 2,000 years old, Cardiff Castle has served many purposes over the years, from a Roman fort, a Victorian palace, and a bomb shelter durring WWII. This only scratches the surface of the history housed behind these walls. Pictured here is the Keep of the Castle, which offers a stunning view of the rest of the city. this stop is a must for anyone infatuated with history and architecture.

Address: Castle St, Cardiff CF10 3RB, UK

Coffee Baker

The Arcades of Cardiff house some of the most memorible experiance for a rainy day, which happens a lot. The Royal Arcade houses Baker Coffee, a wonderful place to warm up and hide out from the weather with a delicious cup of coffee.

Address: 1-13 Castle Arcade, Cardiff CF10 1BU, UK

Cardiff Bay

Cardiff Bay has a lot of historical relevance in Wales, largely centered around mining. Eight the ability to export through the bay, this drove the mining community of the country. Cardiff Bay was also responsible for a large export of coal during World War I. In present day, Cardiff Bay is home to many attractions such as the Camber for Welsh Parliament, the Millennium Centre, beautiful wetland, museums and public spaces such as the Roald Dahl Pass.

Address: Bute Pl, Cardiff CF10 5AL, UK

Hay-on-Wye

Located in the upper region of the Brecon Beacons National Park, Hay-on-Wye is home to the annual Hay Literary Festival and many bookshops. This small, rural Welsh town is home to 20 book stores, and even has shops that are solely dedicated to a specific genre of books. This picturesque town also houses the Hay Castle, which has sadly sustained damage in a fire over the years, but still stands proud with its interesting history. This little town is the perfect escape for a quiet few days after time in the city.

Address: 44 Lion St, Hay-on-Wye, Hereford HR3 5AA, UK

Tintern Abbey

Located closer to the Bristol Channel, Tintern Abbey is the remains of an old Cistercian Monastery that dates back to the 12th century. Tintern Abbey is what was one a stunning effort of Gothic architecture that stood for centuries until it was abandoned and started to dissolve into ruins.
